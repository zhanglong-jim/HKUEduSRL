The effective use of one’s study time is essential to academic success because insufficient time compels expediency the very antithesis of self-regulated learning. Below listed some commonly used strategies:

  1. Setting regular study periods. By setting aside certain hours each day, studying requires less daily planning and becomes more habitual.
  2. Setting realistic goals. Many students tend to underestimate the amount of time needed to complete an assignment and thus need to overestimate the time necessary until they are certain of their estimates.
